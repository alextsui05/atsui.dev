2020-05-25 New habits back home

I made my way home to San Jose at the beginning of the month, and after finishing up my two week quarantine and monitoring my temperature regularly, I have a neat time series to show off.

Actually, I did go into this with the idea of being disciplined and making a nice time series, but I learned pretty quickly that there’s a lot of factors that can lead to a combo breaker. First of all, it didn’t help that I decided to take a temperature reading before every meal. I just wanted three data points spaced throughout the day, and meals are naturally spaced out and so are easy to align to. On the other hand, when I’m busy and food is on the table, I’ll sometimes miss the part where I’m supposed to take my temperature. Although I’m pretty good about sticking to the routine, taking the temperature reading with an electric thermometer isn’t immediate, so I think there is at least some resistance when, say, the food is already on the table and I have to get going. Other times I do remember but opt to sit down to eat right away and take my temperature afterwards, but I occasionally end up forgetting to do so. This compounds with the fact that the data entry is manual. I basically key in the timestamp and temperature into a spreadsheet, and there’s manual work. From the point of view of removing factors that could decrease retention, I think this illustrates the importance of reducing friction in the data collection process. At least until the habit is formed, at which point you’re golden.

Speaking of habits formed, I noticed that since March when I made a conscious decision to adjust my daily behavior (put on a mask when going out, etc), I have picked up a few good habits. For example, I am washing my hands more frequently. This is not only for the case of having come home from the outside, but also everyday situations like after using the toilet. I used to never really wash my hands beyond wetting my hands and wiping off with a towel, but now I actually rub my palms with my fingertips, scrub under my nails, and really give my hands a thorough treatment. I’ve also started flossing and rinsing with a fluoride mouthwash because I’m aware that it’s been over a couple of years now since I’ve seen the dentist, and though I’d been meaning to get myself into a dentist’s office, the recent developments have de-prioritized that, although the priority of dental hygiene remains. I’ve also been opting to eat fruits and veggies, incorporating them into meals when I can. Definitely trying to take all the preventative measures I can on my end to maintain good health.
